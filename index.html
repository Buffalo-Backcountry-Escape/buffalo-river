Float on the River

Take a canoe or kayak out on the water and experience the sheer beauty of the Buffalo River. As you navigate its crystal-clear waters, you'll find yourself surrounded by towering limestone bluffs adorned with lush greenery, creating a scenic backdrop that feels like something out of a dream. Drift along at your own pace, allowing the gentle current to carry you downstream while you soak in the still atmosphere of this natural wonderland. Along the way, pause to dip your toes in the cool, refreshing waters or perhaps even take a swim in one of the inviting swimming holes scattered along the riverbanks. So grab your paddle, pack a picnic, and embark on a floating adventure that promises to leave you with memories to last a lifetime.

Backcountry Hiking

Embark on a journey of discovery along the breathtaking hiking trails of the Buffalo River. With a landscape carved by time, these trails offer an immersive experience into the heart of nature's beauty. Traverse winding paths that lead through verdant forests, alongside cascading waterfalls, and to panoramic overlooks revealing the river's majestic splendor below. Whether you're a seasoned hiker seeking a challenge or a leisurely stroller looking to reconnect with the outdoors, the Buffalo River trails cater to all levels of adventurers. So lace up your boots, breathe in the crisp, fresh air, and set forth on an unforgettable journey along the hiking trails of the Buffalo River.

About the River

The Buffalo River was designated as America's first national river in 1972. It is managed by the National Park Service as the Buffalo National River. The river and its surrounding ecosystem support a diverse array of plant and animal life, including numerous species of fish, birds, and mammals. It is also home to a variety of unique geological features, such as caves and springs. Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect the ecological integrity of the Buffalo River and its surrounding watershed. The National Park Service, along with various conservation organizations and local stakeholders, work to preserve the river's natural resources and recreational opportunities for future generations. Overall, the Buffalo River holds significant cultural, recreational, and ecological importance, drawing visitors from across the country to experience its pristine beauty and outdoor activities.
